# Nusantara Palm Industries — Color Schema

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 2026

---

## Overview

Three color palette recommendations for Nusantara Palm Industries (NPI) landing page, aligned with brand identity: modern industrial aesthetic, sustainability leadership, and Indonesian heritage.

---

## Palette 1: Industrial Emerald

**Theme:** Corporate Sustainability

### Colors

| Role | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Primary | #1a4d3e | rgb(26, 77, 62) | Headers, primary brand, section backgrounds |
| Secondary | #3d5a80 | rgb(90, 90, 128) | Subheaders, accents, trust elements |
| Accent | #c9a227 | rgb(201, 162, 39) | CTAs, highlights, product references |
| Background | #fafafa | rgb(250, 250, 250) | Main canvas, content areas |
| Dark | #1a1a2e | rgb(26, 26, 46) | Footer, text, borders |

### Why It Works

- Deep green anchors the sustainability story
- Slate blue adds corporate credibility for B2B trade
- Gold hints at palm oil commodity value
- Signals "responsible agriculture meets serious business"

### Pros
- Green immediately reads "sustainable/eco-friendly"
- Blue builds trust with international buyers
- Gold adds warmth without looking cheap
- Works well for industrial aesthetic
- Good contrast ratios

### Cons
- Green can feel "overused" in agriculture
- Blue may feel too corporate/generic
- Gold accent needs careful用量 control

## Palette 2: Nusantara Earth

**Theme:** Heritage & Modernity

### Colors

| Role | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Primary | #c45b28 | rgb(196, 91, 40) | Hero, primary brand, CTAs |
| Secondary | #2a5c55 | rgb(42, 92, 85) | Sustainability sections, trust |
| Accent | #d4a24c | rgb(212, 162, 76) | Highlights, icons, product |
| Background | #f5f0e8 | rgb(245, 240, 232) | Main canvas, organic feel |
| Dark | #2c2416 | rgb(44, 36, 22) | Text, footers, industrial grounding |

### Why It Works

- Burnt sienna evokes Indonesian soil and heritage
- Teal adds modern sustainability messaging
- Gold connects directly to palm oil product
- Warm cream replaces sterile white for earthier feel

### Pros
- Unique warmth stands out from typical corporate blues/greens
- Sienna immediately signals Indonesian identity
- Earthy tones align with agricultural roots
- Feels authentic, not "corporate greenwashing"

### Cons
- Sienna may read too casual for serious B2B
- Warm palette harder to make feel "industrial"
- Requires careful balance to not look rustic

## Palette 3: Trade Titanium

**Theme:** Global Trade Professional

### Colors

| Role | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Primary | #0f3460 | rgb(15, 52, 96) | Hero, headers, primary brand |
| Secondary | #4a5568 | rgb(74, 85, 104) | Subheaders, industrial accents |
| Accent | #d97706 | rgb(217, 119, 6) | CTAs, product references, highlights |
| Background | #ffffff | rgb(255, 255, 255) | Main canvas, clean clarity |
| Dark | #0d0d0d | rgb(13, 13, 13) | Text, footers, maximum contrast |

### Why It Works

- Navy dominates global trade and corporate sectors
- Steel gray grounds it in industrial operations
- Amber oil tone directly references palm oil product
- Crisp white maximizes readability

### Pros
- Navy = proven trust in international trade
- Gray adds industrial credibility
- Amber is unmistakable palm oil reference
- Maximum readability and professional appearance
- Safe choice for B2B relationships

### Cons
- Less distinctive than warmer palettes
- Can feel cold/distant
- Sustainability story less obvious (needs green accents)

## Recommendation

### Primary Choice: Palette 2 — Nusantara Earth

For the landing page portfolio showcase, **Nusantara Earth** is recommended because:

1. **Distinctive Identity** — Stands out from typical corporate green/blue palettes
2. **Indonesian Heritage** — Burnt sienna immediately signals Indonesian roots
3. **Sustainability Authenticity** — Teal + gold combo reinforces eco-credentials without clichés
4. **Portfolio Appeal** — Unique color story demonstrates creative thinking
5. **Balanced Warmth** — Professional yet approachable, not cold or generic

### Secondary Choice: Palette 1 — Industrial Emerald

Safe fallback if client or stakeholders prefer more traditional corporate aesthetics.

### Alternative: Palette 3 — Trade Titanium

Best if targeting purely B2B export relationships where trust/safety outweigh distinctiveness.

## Usage Guidelines

| Background | Warm Cream | #f5f0e8 |
| Footer | Dark Coffee | #2c2416 |

### Accessibility Note

All recommended palettes meet WCAG AA contrast requirements (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text). Verify all text/background combinations before implementation.

### Responsive Behavior

- **Desktop:** Full palette as specified
- **Tablet:** Same palette, adjust spacing
- **Mobile:** Same palette, ensure touch targets meet minimum 44x44px
